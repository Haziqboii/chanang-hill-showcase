01 The Heritage

Before the Code,
There Was the Hill

For generations, Chanang Hill stood in quiet reverence as known only to the people who walked its trails barefoot, who read the jungle like a language, and who never needed a website to know its worth.

The Roots

A Hill Known Only by Footprints

Long before GPS coordinates or trail ratings, Chanang Hill was a sacred passage through the Crocker Range, navigated by the Dusun community of Bingkor village through oral tradition and inherited memory.

The Foundation

Hands That Built What Maps Couldn't Show

A group of local hikers carved three distinct trails through the dense landscape, where Trail A, Trail B, and Trail C were created entirely through community effort. The summit at 718 metres is no longer unreachable, it is an invitation for all to explore.

The Present

Where Heritage Meets Horizon

Today, Chanang Hill stands as one of Keningau's most promising eco-tourism assets. But the mountain alone wasn't enough. It needed a voice. It needed to be found.

02 The Challenge
01. Discovery Status | UNMANAGED
Fragmented Brand

The lack of an official digital authority leaves the Chanang brand fragmented. This prevents the hill from reaching its full potential as a world-class tourism landmark.

02. Booking Method | MANUAL
Scaling Bottleneck

Inefficient DM-based coordination creates a massive scaling bottleneck. This technical friction limits visitor volume and the potential spending power entering the Bingkor region.

03. Economic Leakage | DISCONNECTED
Invisible Economy

Bingkor's local businesses and Takiding weavers remain digitally invisible. Without a platform, tourists "pass through" without a gateway to discover or support the local economy.

04. Resource Oversight | UNTRACKED
Unmonitored Growth

The absence of a data-managed system means environmental and community impact is unmonitored. This unmanaged growth threatens the long-term prosperity of the entire district.

Bridging the Divide: From Highlands to Digital Excellence

Keningau, in Sabah's interior, sits on an untapped goldmine of tourism potential. While Chanang Hill is a natural masterpiece, its fragmented digital presence leads to Economic Leakage.

A famous hill without a unified platform means that while visitor numbers may grow, the financial profit fails to reach the surrounding Bingkor community. The challenge was to build a digital bridge that captures this value ensuring that the hill's fame directly fuels the prosperity of every local entrepreneur and artisan.

07 Technical Innovation

Built with Rigorous Purpose

Bridging the gap between traditional heritage and modern digital authority through purpose-built innovation.

Interactive 3D Terrain Visualization

An immersive three-dimensional representation of Chanang Hill's trail system, featuring real elevation data, interactive waypoints marking key landmarks, and dynamic terrain rendering. Visitors can explore the trail virtually before arriving, helping them prepare for their hike and set expectations.

User Value Hikers arrive prepared — they know what to expect, reducing safety incidents and improving satisfaction.

Panoramic Virtual Trail Experience

Using high-resolution panoramic photography captured at key points along the trail, visitors can immerse themselves in a 360-degree virtual walkthrough. This feature transforms the browsing experience into a preview adventure, creating excitement and confidence for first-time visitors.

User Value Virtual exploration builds excitement and removes uncertainty for first-time visitors.

Intelligent Visitor Assistant

An AI-powered chatbot that answers visitor queries about trail conditions, difficulty levels, equipment needs, weather, booking availability, and local attractions. It serves as a 24/7 digital concierge, reducing the burden on local operators while ensuring tourists receive accurate, instant information.

User Value Instant answers, 24/7 availability — no waiting, no language barriers.

Fully Responsive Design

Every component of the platform adapts seamlessly from desktop monitors to mobile phones. Given that most tourists browse on their phones while traveling, mobile-first design was a core principle — not an afterthought. The interface remains beautiful and functional at every viewport.

User Value Perfect experience on any device — hikers access information on-the-go.

08 Community Impact

Empowering the Community

This project is not just about technology — it's about creating sustainable opportunities for the people of Keningau.

Employment & Sustainable Livelihoods

By establishing a professional digital presence, Chanang Hill gains significantly higher visibility among the hiking community. This increased foot traffic directly stimulates the local economy, creating a rising demand for experienced local guides and providing sustainable income opportunities for the Keningau community.

Key Outcome Empowering local guides through increased digital visibility and tourism demand.

Bingkor Enterprise & Cultural Preservation

The platform promotes indigenous crafts and local enterprises throughout the Bingkor area, providing direct income to community artisans. By digitizing traditional products like the hand-woven Takiding, it preserves indigenous craftsmanship while opening new global market channels.

Key Outcome Digital visibility for local Bingkor artisans and authentic cultural products.

Bridging the Digital Divide

This project didn't just build a product for the community — it built capacity within the community. The local management team gained hands-on experience operating a modern web platform, managing dashboards, and interacting with AI integration. This approach demystified technology and transferred crucial digital literacy skills.

Key Outcome Tech literacy uplift and long-term digital independence

Trail Running Events & Seasonal Tourism

The digital infrastructure we've built lays the groundwork for future trail running events that could drive seasonal revenue for local homestays, food vendors, and transport operators. With sponsorship and community support, Chanang Hill has the potential to become a recognized trail running destination, creating an annual economic cycle for the community.

Vision Annual trail events generating sustainable seasonal income for the community

10 Future Vision

The Three Pillars of Legacy

Beyond the pilot, we are building a generational vision to safeguard the biological integrity and cultural heritage of Chanang Hill.

Ecological Restoration

1. The Eco-Sanctuary

A definitive long-term reforestation initiative dedicated to preserving the biological integrity of the Crocker Range. This pillar prioritizes the establishment of native seedling banks and zero-waste operational protocols to safeguard endemic wildlife habitats.

Central to this vision is the 'Plant-as-you-Hike' program, allowing visitors to sponsor and personally plant native seedlings in designated restoration zones along Trail B and C, ensuring a lasting environmental legacy.

Digital Innovation

2. The Digital Frontier

The integration of cutting-edge technology to redefine sustainable tourism and trail safety. This framework utilizes WebAR-driven navigation, interactive digital education markers, and real-time IoT environmental monitoring.

These innovations provide a more secure, data-informed, and immersive exploration of the Borneo rainforest while minimizing human impact on sensitive ecological zones.

Social Craftsmanship

3. The Bingkor Enterprise Hub

A localized economic engine designed to build a direct bridge between global tourism and the artisan heritage of Bingkor. This hub facilitates the incubation of youth-led eco-startups, ranging from organic local provisions to digital storytelling, while providing a dedicated platform for master weavers to showcase traditional crafts such as the Takiding.

This model ensures community-wide prosperity and the preservation of indigenous skills for future generations.
